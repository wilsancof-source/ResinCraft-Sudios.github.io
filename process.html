There are no shortcuts in epoxy flooring. Skip a step and the floor fails — it peels, cracks, or lifts. Our process is the same on every project, whether it's 50 m² or 5,000 m². Here's exactly what happens.

The Process

NOTHING GETS
SKIPPED.
NOTHING.

Each step in our process exists because skipping it causes a real, visible problem down the line. We'll walk you through what we do, why we do it, and what to expect at each stage of your project.

01

SITE ASSESSMENT

Before We Price Anything

We come to the site before any quotation is finalised. A floor has to be looked at — photos and measurements aren't enough. We're checking the concrete condition, the moisture level, how the space is used, and whether there's anything that needs to be fixed before a coating goes down.

This visit usually takes an hour or two, depending on the size and complexity of the space. At the end of it, we know exactly what system to specify and how long the job will take. If we find a problem that will affect the floor's performance, we tell you then — not after we've started.

Duration
1–3 hours on site
Includes
Moisture test, substrate check
Output
Written spec + quotation
02

SURFACE PREPARATION

The Most Important Step

This is where most flooring failures come from — poor or skipped preparation. Before any epoxy is applied, the concrete needs to be mechanically opened so the coating can bond properly. We use diamond grinders and shot blasting equipment to achieve the right surface profile.

At the same time, we fill cracks, treat any contaminated areas, and apply a moisture barrier if the readings require it. This stage takes as long as it takes — rushing it to save time always creates a problem later, and we've seen enough failed floors to know that.

Method
Diamond grinding / shot blast
Crack treatment
Injection or filler compound
Profile target
CSP 2–4 depending on system
03

PRIMER APPLICATION

The Bond That Makes Everything Work

The primer is a low-viscosity epoxy that penetrates deep into the prepared concrete surface. It's not cosmetic — it creates the chemical bond between the substrate and the system layers above it. Without a proper primer, the coating sits on top of the floor rather than bonding to it.

We apply primer the same day as the surface preparation, while the concrete is clean and open. The primer is left to cure fully before the next coat goes down. Cutting this time short is another common cause of delamination, and we don't do it.

Type
Low-viscosity penetrating epoxy
Cure time
8–12 hours minimum
Purpose
Substrate bond + pinhole seal
04

SYSTEM INSTALLATION

The Core of the Job

This is where the specified system goes down — the main body coat or coats that give the floor its performance properties. For an industrial floor, this might be a 2–3mm self-levelling epoxy. For a decorative resin, it's the metallic or coloured layer where the aesthetic effect is created.

We mix every batch by weight using calibrated scales, apply at the specified dry film thickness, and check coverage as we go. The conditions on site — temperature, humidity — are monitored throughout to make sure the curing process runs correctly.

Mix method
By weight — calibrated scales
DFT checked
At every pour
Monitoring
Temp & humidity logged
05

TOPCOAT & FINISHING

What the World Sees

The topcoat is the final protective layer — it determines the sheen level, the slip resistance, and the long-term resistance to UV, chemicals, and surface wear. We use polyurethane or polyaspartic topcoats depending on the system and environment.

For decorative floors, this is also the layer that locks in the visual effect. A high-gloss topcoat amplifies metallic finishes; a matte topcoat gives a more natural concrete look. We'll discuss the options during the specification stage so there are no surprises when you see the finished result.

06

INSPECTION & HANDOVER

We Don't Leave Until It's Right

Before we pack up, the floor is inspected thoroughly — adhesion pull tests, DFT readings, visual check for pinholes or any imperfections. We check every edge, every joint, every area that was repaired. If we find something that doesn't meet specification, it gets fixed before handover.

When we hand over, we give you a brief maintenance guide and our contact details. Epoxy is low-maintenance, but it's worth knowing what to use and what to avoid. And if something comes up in the weeks after installation, we're reachable and we'll come back.

Pull adhesion + DFT check

Why It Matters

Most floor coatings that fail don't fail because the product was bad. They fail because the concrete wasn't prepared properly, or the primer was rushed, or someone applied the topcoat before the base coat had cured. These are avoidable problems.

Following the full process adds time and cost upfront. But a floor installed correctly the first time costs significantly less over its life than one that needs emergency repairs or full replacement after a few years.

Common Questions

THINGS PEOPLE
USUALLY ASK.

How long does the full process take?
+

It depends on the size and condition of the space. A typical 200–500 m² commercial floor takes 3–5 days including preparation. Larger industrial projects may take 1–2 weeks. We'll give you a clear timeline before we start, and we flag anything that could affect it during the site assessment.

Can we use the space during the work?
+

Usually not in the areas being coated. For large spaces, we often section the work so part of the floor remains accessible while the other part cures. This requires planning upfront — let us know if operational continuity is important and we'll design the project schedule around it.

What if the concrete is in poor condition?
+

Most concrete can be coated once it's properly prepared. Cracks get filled, weak spots get stabilised, and damp areas get treated with a moisture-tolerant primer. The key is identifying the issues before the job starts — which is exactly what the site assessment is for. In rare cases where the substrate is genuinely unsuitable, we'll tell you before we start.

How long will the floor last?
+

A properly installed epoxy floor in a commercial or industrial environment typically lasts 10 years or more with normal cleaning and maintenance. Decorative resin floors in residential settings often last longer because the wear is lighter. The biggest factor is how well the surface is looked after — avoiding abrasive cleaning products and addressing small chips or scratches early makes a significant difference.

Does the floor need any maintenance after installation?
+

Epoxy floors are low-maintenance — regular cleaning with a neutral pH cleaner is all most floors need day-to-day. For high-traffic industrial floors, we recommend a periodic inspection to catch any minor wear before it becomes a bigger issue. We offer scheduled maintenance visits for clients who want ongoing support — it's worth considering for commercial and industrial spaces that see heavy daily use.

Can you coat over an existing epoxy floor?
+

Sometimes, yes — but it depends on the condition and adhesion of the existing coating. We'll test it during the site visit. If the old coating is still well-bonded, it can sometimes be abraded and recoated. If it's lifting or delaminating, it needs to come off first. We won't coat over a floor that will fail — it would just create a more expensive problem later.
