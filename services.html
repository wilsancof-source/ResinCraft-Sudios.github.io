Before Treatment
04

CONCRETE
REPAIR & PREP

Réparation Béton · إصلاح الخرسانة

A coating is only as good as what's underneath it. Most flooring failures happen because someone skipped the preparation — they coated over cracks, damp concrete, or a dusty surface and hoped for the best. We don't do that. Before any epoxy goes down, the substrate gets properly assessed and treated. This includes crack injection, levelling compounds, moisture tests, and mechanical grinding.

Crack injection & filling — structural and cosmetic cracks treated before coating.
Shot blasting & grinding — opens the concrete pores for proper epoxy adhesion.
Moisture mitigation — damp substrates treated with specialised barrier primers.
Surface levelling — high spots and dips corrected to achieve a flat, even surface.

ESD / Anti-Static
07

ANTI-STATIC
ESD SYSTEMS

Systèmes ESD · أنظمة مضادة للكهرباء الساكنة

Electrostatic discharge is a serious risk in military facilities, electronics manufacturing, data centres, and aviation environments. A single uncontrolled static discharge can damage sensitive equipment or trigger hazardous ignition. Anti-static ESD flooring systems provide a controlled path to ground, keeping static charge within safe limits. These are specified and installed to IEC 61340 standards.

ESD compliant — installed to IEC 61340-4-1 specification with documented resistance readings.
Conductive or dissipative — system selected based on the specific sensitivity of the environment.
Military & defence ready — suitable for armouries, communications rooms, and maintenance bays.
Test certification included — resistance readings measured and documented at handover.

Heavy Duty System
08

EPOXY MORTAR
SYSTEMS

Mortier Époxy · ملاط الإيبوكسي

Epoxy mortar is the heaviest-duty flooring system we install. Typically applied at 6–12mm thickness, it's designed for environments with extreme mechanical loading, thermal shock, heavy chemical exposure, or direct food and beverage contact. Food processing plants, commercial kitchens, chemical storage facilities, and breweries are typical applications. The system can be sloped to drain, textured for slip resistance, and coved at the walls for full hygiene compliance.

6–12mm thickness — handles extreme impact and point loading that thinner systems can't.
Thermal shock resistant — suitable for environments with hot water washdown or steam cleaning.
Food-safe certified — materials comply with food contact standards for processing facilities.
Fall-to-drain installation — floor graded and sloped for drainage as part of the system.

PERFORMING.

A well-installed epoxy floor doesn't need much — but it does benefit from occasional attention. Over time, heavy-use areas can show light surface wear, and a small scuff caught early is much easier to deal with than one that's been left. For clients who want peace of mind, we offer scheduled maintenance visits that keep the floor looking and performing as it should. It's a straightforward service — an inspection, light treatment, and a report. Most clients find it pays for itself by extending the life of the floor significantly.

ANNUAL INSPECTION

A full walk-over inspection of the floor surface, checking for wear, edge lifting, or any areas that need attention. Written report included.

Light mechanical cleaning, re-seal of any worn zones, and topcoat touch-up where needed. Keeps the floor looking new without a full resurfacing.

Every 2–3 years, depending on traffic
